# Territorial Disputes

Wild wolf packs are naturally territorial. When two pack leaders meet, they will negotiate a resolution based on their personalities.

## Possible Outcomes

Every encounter has three potential outcomes:
1. **War**: The leaders (and their packs) fight for dominance. This can be non-lethal (yielding) or fatal based on world settings.
2. **Merge**: One leader recognizes the other's dominance and joins their pack.
3. **Retreat**: Both packs agree to separate peacefully and maintain their own territory.

## Personality Influence

The chance of each outcome depends on the personalities involved:
- **Aggressive Leaders**: High chance of War. They rarely back down and prefer to absorb rivals by force.
- **Normal Leaders**: Balanced approach. They will fight if provoked but prefer merging if the rival is more dominant.
- **Pacifist Leaders**: High chance of Retreat. They avoid conflict at all costs and rarely start wars.

## Leadership Hierarchy
When two packs merge, the new leader is chosen by personality rank:
**Aggressive > Normal > Pacifist**

If both leaders have the same personality, the stronger (healthier) wolf usually takes charge.
